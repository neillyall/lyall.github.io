Course Outline and Some Class Notes

For the most part we shall (unashamedly) follow closely the appropriate sections of the references listed below.
  • Overture: Ramsey Theory (Math 8440 course notes from Spring 2011)  [we will not discuss this in this class]
    • Monochromatic Corners and van der Waerden for 3APs (following Graham and Solymosi)
  • Introduction
    • The Erdos-Turan constant and a discussion of known bounds (including Behrend's construction)   [Lecture 1]
      • Varnavides Theorem (quantitative form)
  • Roth's Theorem (Math 8910 course notes from summer 2012) [we will only recall the "classical" Fourier analytic proof in this class] 
    • Meshulam's Theorem (the model setting for the Fourier analytic argument)
    • Roth's "classical" Fourier analytic argument   [Lecture 2]
    • Further Fourier analytic arguments
      • Bogolyubov's method
      • Heath-Brown/Szemeredi
      • Bourgain
    • Some "soft" proofs of Roth's theorem
      • Croot and Sisask's argument using Varnavides (essentially following their paper "A New Proof of Roth's Theorem")
      • Triangle removal proof
        • The Regularity Lemma
      • Modern Combinatorial proof via an "arithmetic regularity lemma"
      • Ergodic theoretic proof
  • Szemeredi's Theorem (for 4APs)
    • Roth's theorem revisited    [Lecture 3]
      • An example of a (linearly) uniform set with many more 4APs than expected
    • Gowers uniformity norms and 4APs    [Lecture 4]
    • Gowers Inverse Argument
      • Introduction/Overview and a motivating example    [Lecture 5]
      • A (local) Inverse Theorem for the U^3-norm    [Lectures 5 & 6]
      • (Local) quadratic bias leads to a density increment   [Lecture 7]
    • Supplements
      • The Weyl Inequality and Heilbronn Property   [Lecture 8]
        • Here is a presentation with an alternative proof of Claim 1
  • The Balog-Szemeredi-Gowers Theorem (essentially following Chapter 4 of Ben Green's 2009 notes)
    • Introduction and reduction to a Proposition on Bipartite Graphs   [Lecture 14]
      • Proof of the Proposition on Bipartite Graphs  [Lecture 15]
    • A simpler proof giving only exponential bounds
  • The Freiman-Ruzsa Theorem
    • Introduction    [Lecture 9]
    • The Freiman-Ruzsa theorem for torsion-free groups
      • Plunnecke's inequality  [Lecture 10]
        • Application: Freiman-Ruzsa theorem for torsion groups (including a discussion of Ruzsa's covering lemma)   [Lecture 9] 
      • Bohr sets and Bogolyubov's lemma   [Lecture 10]
        • Supplement on the structure of the large spectrum: Chang's Structure Theorem (following Green's "Edinburgh Lecture Notes")
      • Structure of Bohr Sets (see Chapter 6 of Ben Green's 2012 notes for a proof of Minkowski's Successive Minima Theorem)   [Lecture 11] 
      • Ruzsa's model lemma (following Ruzsa's proof, see Chapter 6 of Ben Green's 2012 notes for an alternative presentation)   [Lecture 13]
      • Conclusion of the proof    [Lecture 12]
        • Appendix: The variant of Freiman's theorem that we needed in our discussion of 4APs   
    • The Freiman-Ruzsa theorem for abelian groups?
  • More on Bogolyubov's lemma
    • Sums of Random Variables: Khintchine's Inequality and the Marcinkiewicz-Zygmund Inequality
      • Chang's Structure Theorem via Rudin's Inequality (following Green's survey article on "spectral structure")
      • Easy Proof of Chang's Theorem
    • The Croot-Sisask lemma (see below)
    • Sanders' amplification
  • Additive Structure in Sumsets
    • An Introduction and Easy Results (temporary, not really what I want to say... Approximate Groups?)
    • Long Arithmetic Progressions in Sumsets and (Global) L^p-Almost-Periodicity via Random Sampling in Frequency Space
    • Random Sampling in Physical Space and the Croot-Sisask Lemma
  • The Green-Tao Theorem
    • Notes
    • Vinogradov's Theorem (Notes by Alex Rice from a learning seminar at UGA in Fall 2009)
  • Additional Topics
    • Sarkozy's theorem
